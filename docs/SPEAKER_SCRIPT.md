## SLIDE 4: VIBE CODING PHILOSOPHY (3:30–5:00)

*[Gesture to the left side of the slide]*

**"But first—let me take you back to what made this possible. It's called vibe coding."**

*[Pause]*

**"Vibe coding is not about specific tools. It's a philosophy. Three principles."**

*[Point to Step 1]*

**"First: Stay in the terminal. No mouse. No GUI switching. Everything through your keyboard. Because the moment you context-switch, you lose the flow state. And flow state is where the magic happens."**

*[Point to Step 2]*

**"Second: Think in systems. Don't think about syntax. Think about intent. What do you want the code to do? Why? Let the AI handle the how. Describe the problem. Let the machine solve it."**

*[Point to Step 3]*

**"Third: Iterate in seconds. Describe what you need. Review the output. Approve it. Do it again. Stay in flow the entire time."**

*[Gesture to the code block]*

**"It looks like this. You describe intent. The agent reads your existing code, understands the patterns, and proposes an implementation with tests. You review. It's done. And you never broke flow. You never opened a GUI."**

*[Pause]*

**"This mindset—this is what allows AI to become your partner."**

## PRESENTATION TIPS

### **Pacing**
- Speak slowly. Especially during key insights.
- Pause 2–3 seconds after major statements to let them land.
- Pause 1 second between slides.
- Don't rush. 15 minutes is plenty of time.

### **Delivery Style (Steve Jobs)**
- Stand still. Move only when it matters (gesturing to slide content).
- Make eye contact with different parts of the audience.
- Speak conversationally, not formally.
- Let pauses do the heavy lifting.
- Never apologize or say "um."

### **Key Moments to Emphasize**
1. The problem (slide 2): "We've automated everything except ourselves"
2. The journey (slide 3): Three phases show progression
3. Vibe coding (slide 4): The philosophy, not tools
4. My choice (slide 5): Honest reasoning matters
5. Eventyay (slide 6): Proof of concept—tech lead, not coder
6. OpenClaw (slide 8): The solution to the problem
7. The automations (slides 9–11): Show real results
8. Five lessons (slide 14): Takeaways
9. The call to action (slide 14): "Go automate something. Today."

### **Tone Throughout**
- Confident but not arrogant
- Honest about struggles and gaps
- Conversational, like talking to friends
- Authentic emotion—this is a real journey, not a sales pitch

### **What NOT to Do**
- Don't ask the audience questions
- Don't pause for applause
- Don't read directly from slides
- Don't rush to the next slide
- Don't apologize or self-deprecate
- Don't make excuses

### **Practice**
- Time yourself. Aim for 15:00–15:30 (leave buffer for natural pauses)
- Practice the pauses. They're part of the rhythm.
- Record yourself once. Watch it. Feel the pacing.
- Practice out loud, not in your head.
- Know your transitions between slides.
