## SLIDE 4: VIBE CODING PHILOSOPHY (3:30–5:00)

*[Gesture left side]*

**"But first—what made this possible. It's called vibe coding."**

*[Pause]*

**"Vibe coding isn't about specific tools. It's a philosophy. Three principles."**

*[Point to Step 1]*

**"First: Stay in the terminal. No mouse. No GUI switching. Everything through your keyboard. Context-switch kills flow state. Flow state is where the magic happens."**

*[Point to Step 2]*

**"Second: Think in systems. Don't think syntax. Think intent. What do you want the code to do? Why? Let AI handle the how."**

*[Point to Step 3]*

**"Third: Iterate in seconds. Describe. Review. Approve. Repeat. Stay in flow."**

*[Gesture to code block]*

**"You describe intent. The agent reads your code, understands patterns, proposes implementation with tests. You review. It's done. Never broke flow. Never opened a GUI. This mindset—this is what makes AI your partner."**

## PRESENTATION TIPS

### Pacing
- Speak slowly, especially during insights
- Pause 2–3 seconds after major statements
- Pause 1 second between slides
- Don't rush. 15 minutes is plenty.

### Delivery Style (Steve Jobs)
- Stand still. Move only when gesturing to content.
- Make eye contact with different audience sections
- Speak conversationally, not formally
- Let pauses do the heavy lifting
- Never apologize or say "um"

### Key Moments to Emphasize
1. The problem (slide 2): "We've automated everything except ourselves"
2. The journey (slide 3): Three phases show progression
3. Vibe coding (slide 4): Philosophy, not tools
4. Eventyay (slide 6): Proof—tech lead, not coder
5. OpenClaw (slide 8): The solution
6. The automations (slides 9–11): Real results
7. Five lessons (slide 14): Takeaways
8. Call to action (slide 14): "Go automate something. Today."

### Tone
- Confident but not arrogant
- Honest about struggles and gaps
- Conversational, like talking to friends
- Authentic emotion—real journey, not sales pitch

### What NOT to Do
- Don't ask audience questions
- Don't pause for applause
- Don't read directly from slides
- Don't rush to next slide
- Don't apologize or self-deprecate
